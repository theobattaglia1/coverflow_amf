01
Honest Assessment
The starting position, the bottleneck, the realistic horizon

You're 5'10", ~162 lb, ~13.5% body fat — about 5–7 lb of fat and 4–6 lb of strategically-placed lean tissue away from the brief. You're not starting over; you're recomposing.

The bottleneck isn't fat — it's delt cap and glute shelf. Without those two structures, getting leaner just makes you look skinnier, not architectural. Every session below is biased toward those two priorities while abs and lean line maintain themselves through volume, tempo discipline, and the existing semaglutide-assisted caloric environment.

The single biggest constraint:
protein under-eating on semaglutide.
Solve that — the lifting is solved here.

Twelve weeks: abs visible at rest in normal lighting, capped delts emerging at the side profile, glutes meaningfully fuller in slim-cut pants.

Twenty-four weeks: full target physique. Vascularity in forearms, V-cut sharp into hips, square chest with clear separation, glutes that read in swimwear.

Band shoulder dislocates × 15
Hold a long resistance band wider than shoulder-width with both hands, palms down. Keep arms straight and lift the band up overhead, then continue back behind you and down to your lower back. Reverse the motion to return to start. Wider grip = easier; narrow it as your shoulders open up.
Scapular wall slides × 10
Stand with your back, head, and elbows pressed against a wall. Bend elbows 90° with backs of hands also touching the wall (think: goalpost arms). Slide arms straight up overhead while keeping all contact points pressed to the wall, then slide back down. If you can't keep contact, that's the point — it's diagnostic of where you're tight.
Banded face pulls × 20
Anchor a band at face height (a power rack pin, a door anchor, or a high pulley with a rope). Grip the band with both hands palms-down, step back to create tension. Pull the band toward your forehead, leading with your elbows wide and high, ending with hands beside your ears. Squeeze rear delts at the end position.
Push-up to down-dog × 8
Start in a standard push-up position. Do one push-up. At the top, push your hips back and up into downward dog (legs as straight as comfortable, head between arms, heels reaching toward the floor). Hold one breath, then return to push-up position. That's one rep.
Empty bar incline press × 10 (slow tempo)
Just the Smith bar — no plates. Set the bench at 30°. Press with a deliberate 3-second eccentric (lowering) and a 1-second pause at chest before pressing back up. The point is to groove the movement pattern, not to fatigue. Treat it like rehearsal.

01 Smith Incline Press 30° 4 × 8·3-1-X-1·90s rest·RPE 8
Drive elbows to wall behind you
How to do it

Set bench at 30° (the lower notch, not 45°). Lie back, position eyes directly under the bar. Grip the bar just outside shoulder width and unrack with a slight twist. Lower the bar slowly to the upper chest just below the collarbone, pause briefly, then press up and slightly forward — like you're trying to push the wall behind you, not the ceiling. Keep ribs locked down throughout (no big arch like flat bench).

Suggested weight
135–155 lbon the bar
Smith bars typically weigh 15–25 lb. Add to your final number. Push to 165+ as RPE allows.
02 Cable Crossover (high → low) 4 × 12·2-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Squeeze inner pec at hand cross, hold 2s
How to do it

Set both pulleys at the highest position. Grab a D-handle in each hand, step forward into a slight split stance, and lean your torso forward about 15°. Start with arms out wide at shoulder height, with a slight bend in the elbows that doesn't change during the rep. Pull both hands down and across, meeting at hip level — let your hand cross past your opposite hip. Squeeze the inner pec hard for 2 seconds at the bottom before reversing slowly.

Suggested weight
25–35 lbper side
If 12 reps with the prescribed tempo feels easy, the load is too light. The squeeze is the point.
03 Hammer Strength Shoulder Press 4 × 10·3-1-X-0·75s rest·RPE 8
Pinky high at lockout
How to do it

Sit in the machine and adjust the seat so the handles align with your shoulders. Use the neutral grip if available. Press straight up while keeping ribs stacked over hips — no back arch. At lockout, pinky should be slightly higher than thumb. Lower under control to shoulder height; don't drop below, that recruits the front delts incorrectly and stresses the rotator cuff.

Suggested weight
50–70 lbper side
Plate-loaded HS machines vary. Total external load typically 100–140 lb plus carriage weight.
04 DB Lateral Raise (palm down, slight forward angle) 5 × 12·2-1-1-1·45s rest·RPE 8
Lead with the elbow, not the dumbbell
How to do it

Stand tall, dumbbells at your sides, palms facing in. Tilt your torso forward about 10° (not perfectly upright). Lift the dumbbells out to the side, leading with the elbow — pinky should be slightly higher than thumb at the top. Stop just below shoulder height; going higher recruits traps and ruins the lift. Lower under control with a 1-second pause at the bottom (no rest at the bottom — start the next rep).

Suggested weight
12–18 lbDBs
99% of people overshoot here. If you're using 25s with momentum, drop to 15s with strict tempo — you'll feel it more.
05 Cable Y-Raise on incline bench novelty 3 × 15·2-0-1-2·45s rest·RPE 7
Long lever, eyes follow hands
How to do it

Set an incline bench to 45°. Grab two D-handles attached to low pulleys (one cable on each side) and lie face-down on the bench. Start with arms hanging straight down toward the floor. Raise both arms up and out into a "Y" shape — like making a touchdown signal. Squeeze the upper back at the top, then lower slowly. Keep arms long throughout (slight elbow bend only).

Suggested weight
5–10 lbper side
Tiny weight, big result. This trains the rear/medial delt junction — the angle that creates the 3D cap.
06 Overhead Cable Tricep Extension (rope) 3 × 12·3-1-1-1·60s rest·RPE 8
Elbows tight, full lockout
How to do it

Set the cable to high position, attach a rope. Face away from the cable, grab the rope and step forward so your arms are fully extended overhead with the rope behind your head. Lower the rope behind your head by bending only at the elbows — elbows stay pointed forward (not flaring out). Extend back up to full lockout, separating the rope at the top to engage the triceps fully.

Suggested weight
40–55 lb
If your elbows flare, the weight's too heavy — drop and reset.
07 isoCable Lateral Raise Hold (10° below shoulder) 3 × 30s·30s rest
Ribs down, no shrug
How to do it

Set a cable to the lowest position, attach a D-handle. Stand with the cable on the side opposite to your working arm (so the cable runs across the front of your body). Lift the handle out to the side until your arm is just below shoulder height (about 10° below parallel — not all the way up). Hold for 30 seconds without shrugging. Switch arms.

Suggested weight
10–15 lb
Should burn by second 15 and shake by second 25. If you're stable through the whole hold, go heavier.

Warmup / 5 min — tap any item for instructions
Hip CARs × 5 each
"Controlled Articular Rotations." Stand on one leg, hold a wall for balance. Lift the other knee to hip height, then circle it outward (knee opens to the side), then back behind you, then return to start. Make the slowest, biggest circle you can with full hip range. Reverse direction. Five each direction per leg.
Glute bridge × 15 (3-second top hold)
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor about hip-width apart. Drive through your heels to lift your hips up until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes hard at the top and hold for 3 seconds. Lower under control. The 3-second hold is non-negotiable — that's where the warmup activation happens.
90/90 hip switches × 8 each
Sit on the floor with both knees bent at 90°. One leg in front (knee out, foot back), the other to the side (also 90°). Without using your hands, rotate your hips to switch sides — front leg becomes side leg, side leg becomes front. Move slowly, sit tall. Eight switches each side.
Goblet squat hold (10kg DB) × 30s
Hold a 10kg dumbbell vertically against your chest, both hands cupping the top weight. Squat down to a comfortable depth (thighs parallel or below), keeping torso upright and elbows pointing inside knees. Hold the bottom position for 30 seconds. Use your elbows to gently push your knees outward to open the hips.
Lateral band walk × 15 each direction
Loop a mini-band around both legs, just above the knees (or around the ankles for harder). Drop into a quarter squat with feet hip-width apart. Step laterally one direction for 15 steps, keeping tension on the band the whole time (don't let your feet come together). Reverse and walk back 15 steps the other direction.

01 Barbell Hip Thrust 4 × 8·2-2-X-1·90s rest·RPE 8
Chin tucked, ribs down, heel drive
How to do it

Sit on the floor with your upper back braced against a bench (the bench should hit just below shoulder blades). Knees bent, feet flat about hip-width. Roll a loaded barbell over your hips — use a thick pad or Hampton bar. Drive through your heels to lift hips up until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes hard at the top, hold 2 seconds. Lower under control. Tuck your chin (look at hips, not ceiling) and don't hyperextend lower back.

Suggested weight
185–225 lb
Anchor lift — track this religiously. Most people can quickly progress to 275+ here. The top hold is what builds the shape.
02 Dumbbell RDL 4 × 10·4-1-X-0·75s rest·RPE 8
Long spine, push hips to wall behind
How to do it

Hold a dumbbell in each hand, standing tall with feet hip-width apart. Slight bend in knees that doesn't change during the rep (it's a hinge, not a squat). Push your hips backward like you're closing a car door with your butt while lowering the DBs along the front of your thighs. Keep your back flat. Stop when you feel a deep stretch in your hamstrings — usually mid-shin. Drive your hips forward to stand back up.

Suggested weight
60–80 lbDBs each hand
If grip fails before hamstrings, use straps. The 4-second eccentric is where the hamstring sweep gets built.
03 Bulgarian Split Squat (15° forward lean) 3 × 10 each·3-1-X-1·60s rest·RPE 8
Long stride, vertical shin
How to do it

Stand 2–3 feet in front of a bench. Place the top of your back foot on the bench (laces down). Hold dumbbells at your sides. Lean your torso forward about 15° — this targets the glute over the quad. Lower until your front thigh is parallel to the floor. Drive through the front heel (not the toe) to stand back up. Front shin stays mostly vertical; if your knee shoots far past your toes, your stride is too short.

Suggested weight
30–45 lbDBs each hand
Balance is part of the work. If you can't hit 10 strict reps with 30s, do bodyweight first to nail the pattern.
04 Cable Glute Kickback (single-leg) 4 × 12 each·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
Squeeze and HOLD at top
How to do it

Attach an ankle strap to a low cable. Face the machine with hands on the frame for balance. Strap one ankle. Kick that leg straight back and slightly across the body's center line (so the working glute crosses the midline of your standing leg). Squeeze glute hard at the top, hold 2 seconds. Lower under control. Don't arch your lower back to extend further — the squeeze, not the range, is the rep.

Suggested weight
25–40 lb
If you're using your low back to extend, drop the weight by 10 lb.
05 Hip Abduction Machine (forward lean) 4 × 15·2-0-1-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Forward lean changes the muscle target
How to do it

Sit in the abduction machine with knees against the pads. Instead of sitting upright (which trains the lower glute), lean your torso forward 30°. This shifts the target to the upper glute (gluteus medius) — the muscle that creates the side-shelf look. Press knees apart to full range, pause briefly, return slowly. Don't bounce.

Suggested weight
100–140 lb
Machine ranges vary widely. RPE 9 means you're 1 rep from failure on the last set — go heavy here, this is the upper-glute builder.
06 B-Stance Cable RDL novelty 3 × 12 each·3-1-X-1·60s rest·RPE 7
80% load on the front leg
How to do it

Stand facing a low cable with a rope or D-handle attachment. One foot forward, the other foot 6 inches back with just the toe touching the floor (kickstand position). 80% of your weight on the front leg — the back foot is for balance only. Hinge from the hips, lowering the cable while pushing hips back. Drive through the front heel to return. Keep back flat throughout.

Suggested weight
40–60 lb
Should feel like a focused single-leg RDL with training wheels. If it feels 50/50 between legs, shift more weight forward.
07 isoSingle-Leg Hip Thrust Hold (top position) 3 × 25s each·30s rest
Hips square, no rotation
How to do it

Set up like a standard hip thrust — upper back on bench, knees bent. Extend one leg straight out in front of you (the other foot stays planted). Drive the planted foot down to lift hips up to full extension. Hold the top position for 25 seconds. Critical: keep hips square — don't let one side drop or rotate. Switch legs.

Suggested weight
Bodyweight
Add a plate (25–45 lb) on your hips when 25s feels too easy. Most people don't need added weight for the first 4 weeks.

Cat / cow × 8
On hands and knees, hands under shoulders, knees under hips. Inhale — drop your belly toward the floor and look up (cow). Exhale — round your spine toward the ceiling and tuck your chin (cat). Move slowly and breathe with the motion. Eight full cycles.
Dead hang × 30s
Hang from a pull-up bar with a wide overhand grip. Let your shoulders relax up toward your ears initially, then engage your scapulae (pull shoulder blades down and back) to "active" hang. Hold 30 seconds. Keep breathing throughout. Decompresses the spine and primes the lats.
Scapular pull-up × 8
Hang from a pull-up bar in a passive hang (shoulders shrugged up). Without bending your elbows at all, pull your shoulder blades down and back — your body will rise about 2 inches. Hold for one second, then release back to passive hang. This isolates lat activation and is the most underused movement for V-taper development.
Band pull-apart × 20
Hold a light resistance band straight in front of you with both hands at chest height, shoulder-width apart. Keeping arms straight, pull the band apart by squeezing your shoulder blades together until the band touches your chest. Slow return. Twenty reps.
Banded lat stretch × 30s each
Loop a band over a pull-up bar. Grab the band with one hand, palm down. Step back and drop into a slight squat to create tension. You should feel a deep stretch through the lat (side of your back) and shoulder. Optionally rotate your torso slightly to deepen it. Hold 30 seconds, switch sides.

01 Lat Pulldown (wide neutral, slight back lean) 4 × 10·3-1-X-1·90s rest·RPE 8
Pull elbows to back pockets
How to do it

Use the wide neutral-grip handle (palms facing each other). Sit at the lat pulldown machine, knees secured under the pads. Lean back slightly — about 15° from vertical, no more. Pull the bar down to your upper chest by driving your elbows down and slightly back, like you're trying to put them in your back pockets. Squeeze the lats at the bottom. Return slowly.

Suggested weight
130–160 lb
Anchor lift — track every week. Should be one of the easier loads to progress on.
02 Chest-Supported T-Bar / HS Row 4 × 10·2-1-X-1·75s rest·RPE 8
Lats first, then squeeze mid-back
How to do it

Use a chest-supported T-bar row machine or Hammer Strength row. Chest pressed against the pad. Grab the handles with a neutral grip. Initiate the pull by driving elbows down and back — feel the lats engage first — then squeeze the shoulder blades together at the top. Don't shrug. Return slowly without losing chest contact with the pad.

Suggested weight
90–120 lb
For HS row: 1 plate (45 lb) per side is a good starting estimate. T-bar plate-loaded similar.
03 Single-Arm Cable Lat Pullover (low → high) 4 × 12·3-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Long arm, feel the lat lengthen and contract
How to do it

Set the cable to the lowest position, attach a single D-handle. Stand facing the cable, slightly to the side so the cable runs across your body. Grab the handle with the arm farthest from the cable, raise it up overhead with arm fully extended (long arm). Pull the cable down and across your body in a sweeping arc until your hand reaches your opposite hip — keep the arm long throughout (only slight elbow bend). Reverse slowly.

Suggested weight
25–40 lb
Most underused movement for V-taper. The lat stretch at the top is the magic — don't shortchange it.
04 Reverse Pec Deck (pinky-up grip) 4 × 15·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Lead with pinky, hold contraction
How to do it

Sit facing the pec deck (chest against the pad). Grab the handles with a pinky-up grip (thumbs facing each other, pinkies pointing up). This grip emphasizes the rear delts and protects the rotator cuff. Pull the handles back and out, leading with your pinkies. Hold the contracted position for one second. Return under control. Don't recruit traps.

Suggested weight
80–110 lb
Rear delts respond to high reps. RPE 9 means the last 2–3 reps are a grind.
05 Eccentric-Only Chest-Supported Row novelty 3 × 6·5-0-X-0·60s rest·RPE 8
5-second negative every rep
How to do it

Same setup as the chest-supported row. Use a slightly heavier weight than your normal working set (about 20–30% more). Pull the handles up explosively (1 second), then resist the eccentric — lower the weight over a full 5-second count. Count it out: "one Mississippi, two Mississippi..." That's one rep. Six reps total per set.

Suggested weight
110–145 lb
Heavier than your normal CS row because the eccentric is the only working portion. If you can't hold the 5-second count on rep 5–6, the weight is too heavy.
06 Incline DB Curl (arms behind body line) 3 × 10·3-0-1-1·60s rest·RPE 8
No swing, supinate at the top
How to do it

Set an incline bench to about 60°. Sit back, dumbbells in each hand hanging straight down at your sides. Critical detail: let your arms hang fully so they're slightly behind your body line. From this stretched position, curl the dumbbells up — supinate (rotate palms up) as you curl. No swinging, no shoulder movement. Squeeze biceps at top. Lower under control all the way back to the stretched start.

Suggested weight
22–30 lbDBs
Goes much lighter than standing curls because the stretched position eliminates momentum entirely.
07 isoDead Hang (active scap) 3 × max time·60s rest
Active scap, not passive
How to do it

Hang from a pull-up bar with a wide overhand grip. Engage your scapulae — pull your shoulder blades down and back so you feel your lats fire. This is "active hang," not letting yourself dangle passively. Hold for as long as possible. Track your time and aim to beat it the next session.

Suggested weight
Bodyweight
Targets: 60s by week 4, 90s by week 8, 2 min by week 12. Hangs build grip + lat endurance + decompress the spine.

Band pass-through × 15
Same movement as the Day 1 dislocates. Hold a band wider than shoulder-width, palms down. Keep arms straight and pass the band overhead and down behind you, then back. Wider grip is easier — narrow it as your shoulders open.

Stand on one end of a light band, hold the other end in one hand at your side. Lift your arm out to the side with the band creating tension. Twenty reps each arm. Activates the medial delt before loading it heavily.

01 Seated DB Shoulder Press (neutral grip) 4 × 8·3-1-X-0·75s rest·RPE 8
No arch, ribs stacked over hips
How to do it

Set an adjustable bench fully upright. Sit with a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height, palms facing each other (neutral grip). Press straight up without rotating the dumbbells. No back arch — keep ribs stacked over hips. Lower under control to ear height. The neutral grip is more shoulder-friendly than pronated.

Suggested weight
50–65 lbDBs each hand
Push up to 70+ over the program. Watch for shoulder cranking — if you feel pinching, drop weight.
02 Egyptian Lateral Raise novelty 4 × 12 each·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
Lean creates the angle that forces the medial delt
How to do it

Stand next to a cable column. Grab a vertical post or rack with your free hand at chest height. Lean your body away from the post so you're tilted at ~30° (your body forms a diagonal line). Grab a single D-handle from the low cable with your other hand — the cable should be on the opposite side of your body from the working arm. From this leaning position, perform a strict lateral raise with the cable arm. The lean removes momentum and forces strict isolation. Switch sides.

Suggested weight
12–18 lb
Will feel ridiculously hard for the load. That's the point — it's the most pure medial delt isolation that exists.
03 Cable Front Raise (rope, low pulley between legs) 3 × 12·2-0-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
Bring rope to forehead height, no higher
How to do it

Set a cable to the lowest position, attach a rope. Straddle the cable so the rope runs up between your legs. Grab the rope with both hands, palms facing each other. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Raise the rope up and out in front of you to forehead height — no higher. As you raise, separate the rope ends so your hands end up wide apart at the top. Lower slowly.

Suggested weight
30–50 lb
Front delts respond to higher reps. Don't go above forehead — that recruits traps.
04 Reverse Cable Fly (single-arm, cross-body) 4 × 15 each·2-1-1-1·30s rest each·RPE 9
Lead with elbow, not hand
How to do it

Set a cable to chest height, attach a D-handle. Stand sideways to the cable column with the cable on your left side. Grab the handle with your right hand, so the cable crosses in front of your body (left to right). Step away to create tension, arm extended across your chest. Pull the handle back and out to the side, leading with your elbow. Squeeze rear delt at the end. Slow return. Switch sides.

Suggested weight
12–20 lb
High reps + short rest. By the last set you'll be cooked. Stay strict.
05 Cable Crossover (low → high, upper-shelf bias) 3 × 12·2-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Hands meet at chin height
How to do it

Inverse of the Day 1 crossover. Set both pulleys at the lowest position. Grab D-handles, step forward into a slight split stance. Start with arms down at your sides, slight bend in elbows. Sweep both hands up and across your body — meeting at chin height. The upward angle hits the upper chest fibers — the "shelf" muscle. Slow return.

Suggested weight
25–35 lbper side
This one builds the chest line that shows above a low V-neck. Don't go too heavy — form > load.
06 isoLateral Raise Hold (DB at parallel) 3 × 30s·45s rest
Pinky just below thumb
How to do it

Hold a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Lift both DBs out to the side until your arms are parallel to the floor — straight out. Hold for 30 seconds. Pinky just below thumb level (slight tilt forward). Don't shrug. By second 20 your delts will scream — hold through it.

Glute bridge × 15
Same as Day 2's warmup. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive through heels to lift hips up to a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes hard at top. Lower under control. Fifteen reps to fully activate glutes before heavy hip thrusting.
Lateral band walk × 12 each direction
Mini-band around legs above knees. Drop into a quarter squat. Step laterally one direction for 12 steps maintaining tension on the band — don't let feet come together. Reverse and walk back. Pre-activates glute medius before lateral work.
Single-leg balance (eyes closed) × 30s each
Stand on one leg with eyes closed. Hold for 30 seconds. Switch legs. Looks easy, isn't. Activates the entire stabilizer chain (foot, ankle, hip, core) and primes you for the single-leg work coming. If you fall, restart the timer.

01 Heavy Hip Thrust (BB or HS Glute Drive) 4 × 6·2-2-X-1·2 min rest·RPE 9
Top hold is non-negotiable
How to do it

Same setup as the Lower A hip thrust, but heavier. Sit on floor with upper back against bench, feet flat, knees bent. Roll a heavy barbell over your hips with a thick pad. Drive through your heels to lift hips to full extension — body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes hard at the top, hold 2 full seconds. Lower under control. Lower reps + heavier weight here for top-end glute strength.

Suggested weight
225–275 lb
Heavier than your Lower A hip thrust — the lower rep range allows more load. Push to 315+ as you progress.
02 Reverse Lunge (DB, deficit on 2" plate) 3 × 10 each·3-1-X-1·60s rest·RPE 8
Step long, drive through heel
How to do it

Stand on a 2-inch plate or low platform with both feet. Hold dumbbells at your sides. Step backward off the plate with one leg, lowering until back knee is just above the ground. The deficit (standing on the plate) increases the range of motion and stretches the front-leg glute. Drive through the front leg's heel to step back up onto the plate. Alternate legs or do all reps on one side first.

Suggested weight
35–50 lbDBs each hand
The deficit makes this harder than a standard reverse lunge. Start lighter than you think.
03 Single-Leg Hip Extension (45° back ext, glute focus) 4 × 12 each·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
Round upper back slightly to take low back out
How to do it

Set up on a 45° back extension machine. Anchor one foot under the foot pads, lift the other leg straight back so only one leg is doing the work. Round your upper back slightly (like a slight slouch) — this de-emphasizes the lower back and emphasizes the glutes. Hinge at the hips to lower your torso, then squeeze the glute of the working leg to come back up. Hold contracted position 1 second.

Suggested weight
25–45 lbplate held at chest
Start bodyweight, add a plate when you can hit 12 strict reps with the slow tempo.
04 Cable Cossack Squat novelty 3 × 8 each·3-1-X-0·60s rest·RPE 8
Heel down on the bent leg
How to do it

Stand sideways to a cable column. Grab a D-handle from a low cable with the hand farthest from the column. Step away to create tension, hold the handle at chest height. With feet wider than shoulders and toes pointed slightly out, shift your weight to the leg closer to the cable, bending that knee while keeping the other leg straight. Heel of bent leg stays down. Drive back up. Eight reps, then switch sides.

Suggested weight
30–50 lb
Lateral plane work. Most people are weak here. The cable adds resistance and helps with balance.
05 Standing Calf Raise (Smith / HS) 4 × 12·3-1-X-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Pause stretched, full extension at top
How to do it

Use a Smith machine with a low platform under the balls of your feet (or a Hammer Strength standing calf machine). Position the bar across your traps. Stand with the balls of your feet on the platform, heels hanging off. Lower your heels as far as they'll go (full stretch — pause 1 second). Drive up onto the balls of your feet to full extension and hold for 2 seconds at the top.

Suggested weight
180–250 lb
Calves are stubborn. Need heavy load + strict tempo + full stretch every rep. The 2-second top hold is what makes them grow.
06 Seated Calf Raise 3 × 15·2-1-1-2·30s rest·RPE 9
Don't bounce
How to do it

Sit on the seated calf machine with the pads on top of your knees. Balls of your feet on the platform. Lower heels into a full stretch (1 second). Press up to full extension on the balls of your feet, pause 2 seconds. Slow lower. Targets the soleus (deep calf muscle), which gives the calf its width.

Suggested weight
70–110 lb
Soleus responds to high reps + slow tempo. Bouncing reps don't build calves.
07 isoWall Sit at 90° 3 × 45s·60s rest
Back flat, knees over ankles
How to do it

Stand with your back against a wall. Walk your feet out and slide your back down the wall until your thighs are parallel to the floor (90° at the knees). Knees should stack directly over ankles — adjust your foot position if your knees jut forward. Press your entire back flat against the wall. Hold for 45 seconds.

Suggested weight
Bodyweight
Hold a DB at chest level (10–25 lb) when 45s feels easy. Quad burner that doesn't require any equipment beyond a wall.

Aesthetic Priorities
The 5 you never skip — the 5 you actively avoid — the 30-min emergency

→ NEVER SKIP

  1. DB Lateral Raise
    In some form, 4–5 sessions a week. Without delt cap, the silhouette doesn't exist.
  2. Hip Thrust
    Single biggest contributor to the glute shelf. The reference images all have it.
  3. Romanian Deadlift
    Hamstring sweep + glute origin. The line that separates ass from hamstring in shorts.
  4. Smith / HS Incline Press
    Upper-pec shelf is what makes the chest read "square" instead of "round."
  5. Cable Rope Crunch
    Abs need direct hypertrophy work to show at rest, not just be strong.

× ACTIVELY AVOID

  1. High-Bar Back Squat
    Builds quad sweep + trap thickness that destroys the silhouette. RDL hits glutes/hams better.
  2. Heavy Barbell Shrugs
    Yoked traps shorten the neckline and make shoulders look narrower.
  3. Flat Barbell Bench (as primary)
    Builds lower-chest droop, not upper shelf. Use sparingly if at all.
  4. Heavy Barbell Curls
    Bicep mass without shape. You want lean arms with longer bellies. Cable + DB only.
  5. Olympic Lifts
    Explosive trap and upper-back development that fights the V-taper.

Recovery + Lifestyle
Sleep, mobility floor, steam room, rest day. The non-negotiables.
Sleep floor
7 hours, 5+ nights/week. Target 7.5 hours, 6+ nights. Phone out of bedroom by 11pm. Room 65–68°F. No alcohol within 3 hours of bed.
Why sleep matters most
Semaglutide + Vyvanse + heavy training = elevated cortisol baseline. Sleep is the only lever big enough to keep cortisol from sabotaging glute and delt growth. Skip sleep, lose the silhouette.
Mobility floor
10 min daily — same 5 movements: 90/90 hip switches × 10 each · world's greatest stretch × 5 each · cat/cow × 10 · couch stretch × 60s each · banded shoulder dislocates × 15. Do it while coffee brews. Habit, not session.
Steam room protocol
3–4 sessions / week · 15 min · post-lift or evening. Hydrate 16 oz electrolyte before + after. Finish with a 60–90s cold shower for the contrast effect. Equinox doesn't have a sauna; steam is the swap.
Caffeine
Cap 200mg before noon, none after. Vyvanse already does the lifting. Pre-workout caffeine only when sleep was < 7h.
Rest day
5,000–8,000 steps · mobility floor · big real-food meal · one maintenance thing (steam / massage gun / foam roller) · 8 hours sleep target. DO NOT make rest day a "catch up on cardio" day. Rest is the work.
