03
Warmup
5 min / glute activation before heavy hip thrust
→ Why this warmup matters

A cold glute does not contract under 250 lb of barbell. It guesses. Five minutes of targeted activation means the first hip thrust set actually trains the glute instead of dumping the load to the lower back and quads.

Glute bridge × 15 (bodyweight, slow)
Lie on back, feet flat, knees bent. Drive through heels and squeeze glutes to lift hips to a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze hard at top for 2 seconds. Lower. Every rep: squeeze intentionally, not just "lift." This is your glute activation primer before loading.
Banded clamshell × 15 each
Light resistance band above knees. Lie on side, hips stacked, knees bent 90°. Keeping feet together, rotate top knee up toward ceiling like a clamshell opening. Don't let your hips roll back. Squeeze glute med at top. Switch sides. Activates the hip abductor complex you'll need for the Cossack squat and wall sit.
Hip 90/90 stretch × 45s each position
Sit on floor with both legs bent at 90 degrees — one in front, one to the side. Sit tall, both hips pressing toward floor. Lean slightly forward over the front knee to feel the stretch in the rear hip. Switch legs. Opens the hip capsule before heavy hip hinge loading.
Bodyweight reverse lunge × 10 each
Step one foot backward, lower until rear knee nearly touches the floor. Keep front shin vertical. Drive through the front heel to return. Ten each side. Rehearses the lunge pattern you'll do loaded with deficit. Also confirms both hip flexors are warm before you add a 2-inch plate deficit.
Empty-bar or light hip thrust × 10
Barbell only, or just body weight on the bench. Shoulders across bench, feet flat, bar across hips (pad on). Drive hips up to full extension, squeeze glutes at top. Ten reps slow. This is the most important warmup movement of the week — you're grooving the exact pattern you're about to load to 225+ lb.

Working Sets
Tap + on any lift for instructions and starting weight

Tempo key — A-B-X-D = eccentric · bottom pause · concentric (X=explosive) · top pause

01Heavy Hip Thrust (barbell, 2-min rest) 4×6·2-2-X-1·2 min rest·RPE 9
Drive through heels — if you feel it in your quads, reset your feet forward
How to do it

Upper back across a flat bench, bar across hip crease (use pad). Feet flat, shoulder-width apart — toes slightly out, heels directly under knees. Drive through heels, squeeze glutes explosively at the top. Full extension: hips, knees, and shoulders in a straight line. Hold 1 second at top. Lower slowly — 2-second descent, 2-second pause at the bottom. RPE 9 — this should be heavy. Two full minutes between sets.

Starting weight
225–275 lb
Primary strength tracker of the program. Log every session. Goal: 315 lb for reps by Week 16. Add 5–10 lb when all 4×6 are clean.
02Reverse Lunge (DB, 2" deficit on plate) 3×10 each·3-1-X-1·75s rest·RPE 8
Front shin stays vertical — if it drifts forward, your step is too short
How to do it

Stand on a 2-inch plate or small step with both feet. DB in each hand. Step back off the plate with one foot, lowering rear knee to just above the floor. The front foot stays on the plate — this is the deficit that deepens the front-leg glute stretch. Drive through front heel to return. Front shin should be near-vertical throughout — if it drifts forward, you're loading the knee, not the glute.

Starting weight
35–50 lbeach hand
The deficit makes this harder than a standard lunge. Reduce load if the plate is new for you — the stretched range is more demanding.
03Single-Leg Hip Extension (45° back ext, plate) 4×12 each·2-1-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Round the lower back slightly — you're training glute, not spine extension
How to do it

45-degree back extension machine. One leg hooked in, other leg free — toes of the free leg curled up. Hold a plate at chest. Hinge at the hip, lower torso forward (not down — you're on a 45° pad). Let your lower back round slightly — this maximizes glute stretch instead of spinal erector recruitment. Squeeze glute to return to parallel (not hyperextension). Single-leg changes this from a back exercise to a glute exercise.

Starting weight
25–45 lbplate
5 lb plate is fine to start — single-leg with rounded back is already demanding. Build up through the mesocycle.
04Cable Cossack Squat novelty 3×8 each·3-1-X-0·60s rest·RPE 8
Straight leg foot flat on the floor — not angled up
How to do it

Cable at low position, D-handle. Stand sideways to the machine, feet very wide — wider than squat stance. Hold the cable handle at chest. Shift your weight to the leg farther from the machine, bending that knee and lowering your body toward that heel. The other leg straightens completely — foot stays flat. Drive through the bent-leg heel to return. The cable acts as counterbalance and adds resistance through the lateral pattern.

Starting weight
30–50 lb
Novel movement — start lighter and build over weeks. The mobility demand is often the limiter before the strength is.
05Standing Calf Raise (Smith or HS) 4×12·3-1-X-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Full stretch at the bottom every rep — half reps build nothing
How to do it

Smith machine or Hammer Strength calf raise. Toes on edge of plate/step, heels hanging. Lower until you feel a full stretch in the Achilles and soleus — don't rush this. Press up to the peak contraction, hold 2 seconds squeezing hard. Lower slowly over 3 seconds. Full ROM every single rep. Calves need heavy weight and slow tempo — they're trained every day just walking, so they need more to adapt.

Starting weight
180–250 lb
Go heavy. Calves respond poorly to light weight. If you can do 12 reps without burning, load is too light.
06Seated Calf Raise 3×15·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Seated position hits soleus — a different muscle than standing calf raise
How to do it

Seated calf raise machine or bar across thighs. Toes on edge. The bent knee in seated position isolates the soleus — the lower calf muscle that builds the width and fullness under the gastrocnemius. Same full-ROM rule: full stretch down, full squeeze up. Hold contracted position 2 seconds. 15 reps at RPE 9.

Starting weight
70–110 lb
Lighter than standing because the soleus is smaller. Still needs to be challenging to RPE 9 by rep 15.
07isoWall Sit at 90° 3×45s·30s rest
Knees exactly at 90° — higher is easier but cheats the quad burn
How to do it

Back flat against wall, feet shoulder-width apart and far enough from wall that your knees are at exactly 90 degrees. Thighs parallel to the floor. Hold for 45 seconds. Arms out or on thighs — not using your hands to push. This is a finishing isometric for the quads and hip flexors after the hip-dominant movements. Three sets, 30 seconds rest between.

Starting weight
Bodyweight
Add a 10–25 lb plate on your thighs when 45 seconds becomes manageable. Or progress to 60 seconds.
05
Finisher
4 rounds / sled glute burnout
→ 4 rounds / 60s rest between rounds

20 yd sled push (heavy) → 20 yd backward sled drag (lean back, drive through heels — pure glute) → 20 yd lateral sled drag each side (abductor chain).

Lighter load than Thursday conditioning — this is a finishing pump, not a conditioning effort. Focus on feeling the glute work, not maximum speed. The three directions hit different parts of the glute complex for a complete pump to close out the session.
