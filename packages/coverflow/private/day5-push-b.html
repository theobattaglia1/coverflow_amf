03
Warmup
5 min / shoulder + core activation
Band pass-through × 15
Same as Day 1 dislocates. Hold a band wider than shoulder-width, palms down, arms straight. Pass overhead and behind you, then reverse. Wider grip is easier. Opens the shoulder capsule before pressing.
Cuban rotation (5 lb DB) × 10 each
Hold a very light dumbbell. Lift elbow out to shoulder height, forearm hanging straight down. From there, rotate forearm up and back so palm faces forward (forearm now pointing up). Reverse. Ten reps. Activates the rotator cuff before overhead pressing — the muscle that stabilizes everything.
Push-up to T-rotation × 6 each side
Do one push-up. At the top, rotate your body to one side, lifting that arm straight up toward the ceiling — T shape. Hold one breath. Return, do another push-up, rotate the other side. Six rotations per side. Opens the thoracic spine for pressing and wakes up the serratus.
Hollow hold × 20s
Lie on your back. Press lower back into the floor and lift both legs and shoulders a few inches off the ground. Arms straight overhead. Body forms a shallow "U." Lower back must stay pressed to the floor — if it lifts, raise your legs higher. 20 seconds. This is also ab pre-activation for the abs block later.
Band lateral raise × 20
Stand on one end of a light band, hold other end in one hand. Lateral raise motion, 20 reps each arm. Activates medial delt with no fatigue cost before the loaded sets.

Working Sets

Tempo key — A-B-X-D = eccentric · bottom pause · concentric (X=explosive) · top pause

01Seated DB Shoulder Press (neutral grip) 4×8·3-1-X-0·75s rest·RPE 8
No arch — ribs stay stacked over hips the whole set
How to do it

Bench fully upright. DBs at shoulder height, palms facing each other (neutral grip — more shoulder-friendly than pronated). Press straight up without rotating the dumbbells. No back arch, ribs over hips throughout. Lower under control to ear height. Neutral grip takes the shoulder joint through a safer arc than the barbell press.

Starting weight
50–65 lbeach hand
Push toward 70+ across the mesocycle. If you feel shoulder pinching, drop 5 lb and check that your grip is neutral not pronated.
02Egyptian Lateral Raise novelty 4×12 each·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
The lean removes all momentum — this is the purest medial delt isolation
How to do it

Stand next to a cable column. Grab a vertical post or rack with your free hand at chest height. Lean your body away from the post at ~30° — body forms a diagonal. Grab a D-handle from the low cable with the other hand (cable should be on the far side of your body from the working arm). From this leaning position, do a strict lateral raise. The lean eliminates all hip swing and momentum.

Starting weight
12–18 lb
Will feel ridiculously hard for the load. That's the entire point — it's the most isolated medial delt movement in the program.
03Cable Front Raise (rope, low pulley between legs) 3×12·2-0-1-2·45s rest·RPE 8
Stop at forehead height — above that recruits traps
How to do it

Cable at lowest position, rope attachment. Straddle the cable so the rope runs up between your legs. Both hands on the rope, palms facing each other. Raise rope forward and up to forehead height — no higher. As you raise, separate the rope ends so hands end up wide apart at the top. Lower slowly. The between-legs setup creates a unique pulling angle that hits the front delt through a longer arc than standard front raises.

Starting weight
30–50 lb
Front delts respond to higher reps. Stop at forehead — going above that turns it into a trap exercise.
04Reverse Cable Fly (single-arm, cross-body) 4×15 each·2-1-1-1·30s rest each side·RPE 9
Lead with elbow — if you lead with hand it becomes a shoulder rotation
How to do it

Cable at chest height, D-handle. Stand sideways to the column, cable on your left. Grab with your right hand so cable crosses in front of you. Step away to create tension, arm extended across chest. Pull handle back and out leading with your elbow. Squeeze rear delt at end. Slow return. Switch sides. Short rest — 30 seconds each side, then immediately switch.

Starting weight
12–20 lb
High reps, short rest, RPE 9. By the last set you'll be working. Stay strict.
05Cable Crossover (low → high, upper shelf) 3×12·2-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Hands meet at chin height — the angle is what builds the upper shelf
How to do it

Opposite of Day 1 crossover. Both pulleys at lowest position. D-handles, split stance, slight forward lean. Start with arms down at your sides. Sweep both hands up and across your body, meeting at chin height. The upward angle specifically loads the upper chest fibers — the "shelf" that shows above a low V-neck. Slow return. Hold the cross for 2 seconds.

Starting weight
25–35 lbper side
This builds the chest line that shows in clothes. Form over load.
06isoDB Lateral Raise Hold (at parallel) 3×30s·45s rest
Pinky just below thumb — don't shrug
How to do it

Hold a dumbbell in each hand. Lift both out to the side until arms are parallel to the floor. Hold for 30 seconds. Pinky just below thumb level (slight forward tilt). Don't shrug into the hold — if traps are activating, drop 2 lb. By second 20 your delts will scream. That's the adaptation signal. Hold through it.

Starting weight
12–15 lbeach hand
Lighter than your dynamic lateral raise. The 30-second hold is the work, not the load.
05
Abs Block
8 min / tri-set × 3 rounds / 60s rest between rounds
→ Abs are trained like any other muscle

Progressive load. Cable crunch weight goes up week over week. The goal is visible abs at rest — that requires direct hypertrophy work, not just being lean.

ACable Rope Crunch (kneeling) 15 reps·3-1-2-0
Arms are hooks — abs do the work, not the shoulders
How to do it

High cable, rope attachment. Kneel facing away from cable. Hold rope at sides of your head, rope hanging over shoulders. Crunch down by curling your spine — chin toward pelvis — squeezing abs. Hold contracted position 2 seconds. Return slowly. Never pull with your arms — they're hooks for the rope. Log the weight and beat it every week.

Starting weight
60–100 lb
Most underloaded ab movement in any gym. Go heavy and progressive.
BHanging Leg Raise (straight if possible) 12 reps·2-0-1-2
Posterior pelvic tilt BEFORE you lift — that's what makes it an ab exercise
How to do it

Hang from pull-up bar. Initiate by tilting your pelvis backward — tuck tailbone forward — before lifting. Then raise legs straight to parallel (or higher). Lower under control. The pelvic tilt at the start engages lower abs first so hip flexors don't dominate. If straight legs are too hard, bend knees. Add a DB between feet when 12 strict reps becomes easy.

Starting weight
Bodyweight
Add 5–15 lb DB between feet when strict reps feel manageable.
CReverse Crunch with Hip Lift 15 reps·2-1-1-1
Lift hips toward the ceiling — not just knees toward chest
How to do it

Lie on back, hands at sides, knees bent 90° feet off floor. Pull knees toward chest while simultaneously driving hips up off the floor — hips lift toward ceiling. Pause briefly at top with hips elevated. Lower slowly. Feet don't touch ground between reps. The hip lift is what separates this from a basic knee tuck.

Starting weight
Bodyweight
The hip lift at top is non-negotiable. That's the lower ab contraction.
