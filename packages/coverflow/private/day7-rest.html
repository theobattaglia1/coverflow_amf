The Rules for Today
Four non-negotiables for a proper rest day
→ The one mistake that kills progress

Treating Sunday like a bonus cardio day. Extra sessions don't build the body faster — they deplete the recovery budget you need for Monday's Push A. This day is not optional.

Walk 5,000–8,000 stepsActive recovery
Easy walking — not a hike, not a run. A 35–50 minute walk around your neighborhood or Runyon. Low-intensity movement increases blood flow to sore muscles and accelerates glycogen replenishment without adding any training stress. The step count is a range: 5k minimum, 8k maximum. Go over 8k and you've crossed into fatigue territory.
10-minute mobility floor workMaintenance
Not yoga. Not stretching for the sake of it. Ten focused minutes — hips, thoracic spine, shoulders — the areas that get compressed during a 5-day lifting week. Instructions below in the mobility section. This is the minimum that keeps your movement patterns functional heading into week 2.
One big real-food mealPriority
Cook something. Or order from a place where you know what's in the food. Sunday is the best day of the week for real food eating — no pre-workout timing pressure, no post-workout window. Just eat well. Protein + vegetables + carbs + healthy fat. Suggested meals below.
8 hours sleep targetNon-negotiable
Sunday night sleep directly determines Monday's Push A quality. GH release, cortisol reset, muscle protein synthesis — all happen overnight. The entire week's training compresses into this one sleep window. Phone out by 10:30pm. This is the highest-leverage action in the whole program.

10-Minute Mobility Routine
Floor work — hips, spine, shoulders in order

Do these on a mat or carpet, in this order. No timer pressure — move at your own pace through the sequence. Total: ~10 minutes.

Thoracic spine rotation on foam roller × 10 each
Place foam roller perpendicular to your spine at mid-back. Support your head, knees bent. Slowly roll up to your shoulder blades, then back to mid-back. At each point, let your elbows open to the sides for rotation. This decompresses the thoracic spine that gets compressed during all the pressing and pulling this week.
90/90 hip switch × 5 each direction
Sit on the floor with knees bent at 90° — one shin in front, one to the side. Rotate your whole lower body to switch which shin is in front (windshield wiper motion). Move through the full range without lifting off the ground. The hip flexors and external rotators worked hard this week — this is their release valve.
Deep squat hold × 60s
Feet slightly wider than hip-width, toes out. Drop into the deepest squat you can with your heels on the floor. Hold on to a doorframe or squat rack if needed. You can shift your weight gently from side to side. Sixty seconds continuous. Opens hips, ankles, and thoracic spine in one movement.
Pigeon stretch × 60s each
From hands and knees, bring one knee forward and place it behind the same-side wrist — shin angled across your body. The other leg extends back straight. Let your hips sink toward the floor. Walk your hands forward and relax your upper body over your front leg. Sixty seconds each side. This is the deepest glute and piriformis stretch — particularly important after yesterday's Lower B.
Child's pose with lat reach × 45s each
From hands and knees, push hips back toward heels. Walk both hands to the right — arms extended, hands wide right. Feel the stretch through the left lat and the left side of your back. Hold 45 seconds. Walk hands to center, then left. Switch sides. The lat stretch is important after Pull A's high volume this week.
Dead bug × 8 each side
Lie on back. Press lower back firmly into the floor. Arms straight up, knees bent 90° — tabletop position. Simultaneously lower one arm overhead and the opposite leg toward the floor. Keep lower back pressed down — don't let it arch. Return. Switch sides. Eight each side. This is a core stability reset after the heavy loading from this week's sessions.
