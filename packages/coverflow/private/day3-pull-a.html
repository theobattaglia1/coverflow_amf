03
Warmup
5 min / lat and scap activation before any pulling
→ Why this warmup matters

Cold lats pull from biceps and traps instead. Scapular work here ensures the first pulldown rep loads the lat, not compensation muscles.

5 movements — tap + for instructions
Cat / cow × 8
Hands and knees, hands under shoulders. Inhale: drop belly toward floor, lift head and tailbone (cow). Exhale: round spine to ceiling, tuck chin and pelvis (cat). Slow, breathing with each rep. Opens the thoracic spine that needs to extend cleanly during rows.
Dead hang × 30s
Hang from pull-up bar, wide overhand grip. Passive hang 5 seconds (shoulders relax up), then pull shoulder blades down and back — active hang, you'll rise 2 inches. Hold 25 more seconds. Decompresses spine and primes the lat engagement pattern you need for pulldowns.
Scapular pull-up × 8
Hang from pull-up bar. Without bending elbows at all, pull shoulder blades down and back — body rises about 2 inches. Hold 1 second, release. Eight reps. This is the best lat activation drill that exists. If your pulldown doesn't feel like the lat is working, you're not doing this.
Band pull-apart × 20
Hold a light band straight in front at chest height, shoulder-width apart. Arms straight. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades together until band touches your chest. Twenty reps, slow return. Activates rear delts and rhomboids before loading them in rows.
Banded lat stretch × 30s each
Loop band over a pull-up bar. Grab with one hand, step back and drop into a slight squat. Feel the stretch through the lat — armpit to hip. Optionally rotate torso slightly to deepen. Thirty seconds each side. A stretched lat contracts harder than a cold one.
04
Working Sets
Tap + on any lift for instructions and starting weight

Tempo key — A-B-X-D = eccentric · bottom pause · concentric (X=explosive) · top pause

01Lat Pulldown (wide neutral, slight back lean) 4×10·3-1-X-1·90s rest·RPE 8
Pull elbows to your back pockets — not your hips, your back pockets
How to do it

Wide neutral-grip handle, palms facing each other. Sit with knees under pads. Lean back 15 degrees — no more. Pull the bar to your upper chest driving elbows down and back toward your back pockets. Squeeze lats at the bottom, hold 1 second. Return slowly. If biceps fail first, lean back slightly more and drive elbows farther back.

Starting weight
130–160 lb
Anchor lift — log every session without exception. Progressive overload here is the primary V-taper driver.
02Chest-Supported T-Bar / HS Row 4×10·2-1-X-1·75s rest·RPE 8
Lat initiates, mid-back finishes — two-phase pull
How to do it

Chest pressed against pad throughout — removes lower-back fatigue. Neutral grip. Initiate by driving elbows down and back (lat first), then squeeze shoulder blades together at top (mid-back finishes). Don't shrug. Don't lift chest off the pad to reach at the bottom. Full stretch without losing chest contact.

Starting weight
90–120 lb
HS row: 1 plate (45 lb) each side is a reasonable start. Adjust by RPE 8.
03Single-Arm Cable Lat Pullover (low → high) 4×12·3-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Arm stays long — feel the lat stretch overhead, contract at hip
How to do it

Cable at lowest position, D-handle. Stand facing cable, slightly to one side. Grab handle with arm farthest from cable, raise it fully overhead — arm extended long. Pull the cable down and across your body in a sweeping arc until hand reaches the opposite hip. Minimal elbow bend throughout. Reverse slowly. The stretch at the top under cable tension is what builds V-taper width.

Starting weight
25–40 lb
Most underused V-taper movement in the plan. Don't go heavy — the stretch is the point.
04Reverse Pec Deck (pinky-up grip) 4×15·2-1-1-2·45s rest·RPE 9
Lead with pinkies — thumbs-leading makes it a trap exercise
How to do it

Sit facing pec deck, chest against pad. Pinky-up grip (thumbs facing each other, pinkies up). This externally rotates the shoulder and redirects load to rear delts instead of traps. Pull handles back and out, leading with pinkies. Hold contracted position 1 second. Return under control. RPE 9 — last 2–3 reps are a grind.

Starting weight
80–110 lb
Rear delts respond to high reps and high effort. The pinky-up grip makes it feel harder at the same weight.
05Eccentric-Only CS Row novelty 3×6·5-0-X-0·60s rest·RPE 8
Count it out loud — one Mississippi, two, three, four, five
How to do it

Same CS row setup. Load 20–30% heavier than normal. Pull up explosively (1 second), then fight the weight down over a full 5-second count. Six reps total. The slow eccentric is where the peak mechanical tension and most hypertrophy stimulus lives. Heavier load + slow negative = significantly more growth signal per set than standard rows.

Starting weight
110–145 lb
If you can't control the descent on rep 5 or 6, drop the weight — the tempo is the entire exercise.
06Incline DB Curl (arms behind body line) 3×10·3-0-1-1·60s rest·RPE 8
Arms stay behind your body the whole set — zero swing
How to do it

Incline bench at 60°. Sit back, DBs hanging straight down behind body line. From the full stretch position, curl upward supinating (rotating palms up) as you go. No shoulder movement. Squeeze at top, lower all the way back to the full stretch. The stretched start removes all momentum and adds loaded stretch stimulus that builds bicep length.

Starting weight
22–30 lbDBs
Much lighter than standing curls. Correct weight = barely completing rep 10 with strict form and zero swing.
07isoDead Hang (active scap, max duration) 3 × max time·60s rest
Shoulder blades down and back — active, not dangling
How to do it

Hang from pull-up bar, wide overhand grip. Pull shoulder blades down and back so lats fire — "active hang." Hold as long as possible. Record time in Hevy. Beat it every week. Three sets, 60 seconds rest between. Builds grip, lat endurance, and decompresses spine after heavy pulling.

Starting weight
Bodyweight
Milestones: 60s / week 4 · 90s / week 8 · 2 min / week 12. Add 10 lb between feet when bodyweight gets easy.

Finisher
4 min / lat + rear delt density — 3 rounds / 45s rest
→ 3 rounds, no rest within round

12 straight-arm lat pulldown (rope, arms long) → 12 face pull (high pulley, rope, to forehead — elbows wide and high) → 12 cable curl (both arms, standing).

No rest within each round. Lat pump stays active. Rear delt hit from a different angle than pec deck. Bicep finisher. Three movements in 4 minutes.
