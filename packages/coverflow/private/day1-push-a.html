03
Warmup
5 min / shoulder prep before any pressing
→ Why this warmup matters

Cold shoulders + pressing = rotator cuff recruitment instead of pec and delt. Five minutes here means your first set of incline press actually trains what it's supposed to train.

5 movements — tap + for instructions
Band shoulder dislocates × 15
Hold a long resistance band wider than shoulder-width, palms down. Keep arms completely straight. Lift the band up overhead and continue back behind you until it touches your lower back. Reverse to return. The wider your grip, the easier — narrow it as your shoulders open. This is your shoulder mobility meter: if it's tight, your delts will compensate on pressing movements.
Scapular wall slides × 10
Stand with your back, head, elbows, and the backs of your hands pressed against a wall — goalpost arms at 90°. Slide your arms straight up the wall overhead while maintaining all contact points. If your elbows or hands come off the wall, that's where your shoulder mobility breaks down. Ten reps, slow and controlled.
Banded face pulls × 20
Anchor a band at face height. Grip the band with both hands palms down, step back to create tension. Pull toward your forehead, leading with elbows wide and high — hands end up beside your ears. Squeeze rear delts at the end position. This activates the muscle that stabilizes your shoulder during pressing and prevents internal rotation breakdown under load.
Push-up to down-dog × 8
Standard push-up. At the top, push hips back and up into downward dog — legs as straight as comfortable, heels reaching toward the floor, head between arms. Hold one breath. Return to push-up position. Eight full reps. Opens the lats and anterior shoulder while waking up the pushing muscles.
Empty bar incline press × 10 (slow tempo)
Smith machine, no plates. Bench at 30°. Three-second eccentric down, one-second pause at chest, press back up. This is rehearsal, not training — you're grooving the movement pattern and feeling where the bar should travel. Ten reps, deliberate. If you feel the bar wants to drift toward your neck, adjust your grip width outward half an inch.

Working Sets
Tap + on any lift for instructions and starting weight

Tempo key — A-B-X-D = eccentric · bottom pause · concentric (X=explosive) · top pause

01 Smith Incline Press 30° 4×8·3-1-X-1·90s rest·RPE 8
Drive elbows to the wall behind you — not the ceiling
How to do it

Set bench at 30° — the lower notch, not 45°. Position eyes directly under the bar. Grip just outside shoulder width. Unrack with a slight twist. Lower slowly to the upper chest, just below the collarbone — pause briefly. Press up and slightly forward. Keep ribs locked down; no big arch like flat bench. The Smith machine's fixed path is the advantage: it lets you focus entirely on the target muscle.

Starting weight
135–155 lbon the bar
Smith bars weigh ~15–25 lb. Add that to your total. Push toward 165+ as RPE allows across the mesocycle.
02 Cable Crossover (high → low) 4×12·2-0-1-2·60s rest·RPE 8
Squeeze the inner pec at cross — hold 2 seconds every rep
How to do it

Both pulleys at highest position. D-handle each hand. Split stance, torso leaning forward 15°. Arms out wide at shoulder height with a slight elbow bend that doesn't change. Pull both hands down and across — let them cross past the opposite hip. Squeeze inner pec hard for 2 seconds at the bottom. Reverse slowly. The 2-second hold is what builds the seam line between the pecs.

Starting weight
25–35 lbper side
If 12 reps with the prescribed tempo feels easy, load is too light — the hold is the hard part.
03 Hammer Strength Shoulder Press 4×10·3-1-X-0·75s rest·RPE 8
Pinky high at lockout — ribs stay stacked
How to do it

Adjust seat so handles align with shoulders. Use neutral grip if available. Press straight up — no back arch, ribs over hips. At lockout, pinky slightly higher than thumb. Lower under control to shoulder height only — don't drop below, that stresses the rotator cuff and recruits front delts incorrectly. Machine removes the stability variable so you can focus purely on delt load.

Starting weight
50–70 lbper side
Plate-loaded HS machines vary. Total external load typically 100–140 lb plus carriage. Adjust by feel — RPE 8 is the target.
04 DB Lateral Raise (palm down, slight forward lean) 5×12·2-1-1-1·45s rest·RPE 8
Lead with the elbow — the dumbbell follows, it doesn't lead
How to do it

Stand tall. Tilt torso forward 10° — not perfectly upright. Dumbbells at sides. Lift out to the side leading with the elbow, pinky slightly higher than thumb. Stop just below shoulder height — going higher recruits traps. Lower under control with a 1-second pause at the bottom before each rep. This is your most important exercise. Five sets, every time, no exceptions.

Starting weight
12–18 lbDBs
99% of people overshoot here. If you're using 25s with momentum, drop to 15s with strict tempo — you'll feel it more and build faster.
05 Cable Y-Raise on Incline Bench novelty 3×15·2-0-1-2·45s rest·RPE 7
Long arms, eyes track the hands up and out
How to do it

Set incline bench to 45°. Grab two D-handles from low pulleys (one cable each side), lie face-down on the bench. Start with arms hanging straight toward the floor. Raise both arms up and out into a Y shape — like making a touchdown signal. Squeeze upper back and rear delts at the top. Lower slowly. Arms stay long throughout (slight elbow bend only). Hits the rear/medial delt junction — the angle that creates 3D cap depth.

Starting weight
5–10 lbper side
Genuinely light. The long lever and cable angle make 8 lbs feel like 20. Don't ego-load this one.
06 Overhead Cable Tricep Extension (rope) 3×12·3-1-1-1·60s rest·RPE 8
Elbows pointed at the floor the entire rep — no flaring
How to do it

Cable at high position, rope attachment. Face away from the cable. Grab rope and step forward so arms are extended overhead with rope behind your head. Lower by bending only at the elbows — elbows stay forward, not flaring outward. Extend back to full lockout, separating the rope ends at the top. The overhead position keeps the long head of the tricep under stretch — that's where the horseshoe shape comes from.

Starting weight
40–55 lb
If elbows flare outward before rep 8, drop the weight 10 lb — form before load on this one.
07 isoCable Lateral Raise Hold (10° below shoulder) 3×30s·30s rest
Ribs down, no shrug — the burn is the point
How to do it

Cable at lowest position, D-handle. Stand with the cable on the opposite side (cable crosses in front of your body). Lift the handle out to the side until your arm is just below shoulder height — hold for 30 seconds. Don't shrug. Switch arms. The cable constant tension at this angle is what makes it different from a DB hold — the resistance stays on the medial delt through the full hold.

Starting weight
10–15 lb
Should burn by second 15, shake by second 25. If you're stable the whole hold, add 5 lb next session.
05
Finisher
4 min / lateral raise mechanical drop set × 2 rounds
→ 2 rounds / 60s rest between rounds

12 DB lateral raise (heavy) → drop weight → 12 DB lateral (lighter, partials in stretch — bottom half of ROM only) → same DBs → 12 DB front raise.

The "partials in stretch" means you only raise to 45° — staying in the stretched portion where the delt is weakest. Quick, strict reps. No rest within the set. This extends the set past failure by changing the mechanical advantage, not the load.
