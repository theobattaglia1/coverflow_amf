03
Warmup
5 min / non-negotiable before any loaded hip hinge
→ Why warmup matters most on Lower A

The hip joint is the most complex joint you're loading today. Cold hip flexors + glutes = compensatory lower back on the hip thrust. Five minutes here prevents injury and doubles your mind-muscle connection on the first working set.

5 movements — tap + for instructions
Hip CARs × 5 each side
"Controlled Articular Rotations." Stand on one leg and hold a wall or rack for balance. Lift the other knee to hip height, then slowly circle the entire leg outward — knee traces a big arc up and out to the side, then behind you, then back to start. Make the circle as large as your mobility allows. Reverse direction. Five full circles each direction, each leg. You should feel the hip socket moving through its full range.
Glute bridge × 15 (3-second top hold)
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart. Drive through your heels to lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze your glutes hard at the top — hold for a full 3 seconds. Lower under control. 15 reps. This isn't just warmup, it's activation — if you rush through these, your first hip thrust set will feel like your lower back is doing all the work.
90/90 hip switches × 8 each side
Sit on the floor with both knees bent at 90 degrees. One leg is in front of you (knee pointing outward), one is to the side (knee pointing sideways). Without using your hands, rotate your hips to switch sides — the front leg swings to the side, the side leg comes to front. Sit tall throughout. Eight switches each direction. This opens the hip rotators and adductors you'll be loading in the Bulgarian split squat and cable kickback.
Goblet squat hold × 30s (light DB)
Hold a 10kg dumbbell vertically against your chest with both hands cupping the top weight. Squat down until your thighs are parallel to the floor or below — keep your torso upright, chest tall. Hold the bottom position for 30 full seconds. Use your elbows to gently push your knees apart. If your heels lift, you need more ankle mobility — do this on a slight incline (heels on a 5 lb plate).
Lateral band walk × 15 each direction
Loop a mini-band just above your knees (or around your ankles for a harder version). Drop into a quarter squat — slight bend, hips back a few inches. Step laterally one direction for 15 steps, keeping tension on the band throughout (feet never come together). Reverse and walk back 15 steps. This pre-activates the gluteus medius — the upper-glute "shelf" muscle. If you skip this, the abduction machine and kickbacks will feel disconnected.
04
Working Sets
Tap + on any lift for instructions and starting weight

Tempo key — A-B-X-D = eccentric · bottom pause · concentric (X = explosive) · top pause

01 Barbell Hip Thrust 4 × 8· 2-2-X-1· 90s rest· RPE 8
Chin tucked, ribs down, heel drive — the top hold is the rep
How to do it

Sit on the floor with your upper back braced against a bench — the edge should hit just below your shoulder blades. Knees bent, feet flat about hip-width. Roll a loaded barbell over your hips — use a thick pad or Hampton bar. Tuck your chin (look at your hips, not the ceiling). Drive through your heels to lift hips up until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze glutes hard at the top and hold for 2 full seconds. Lower under control. The hold is not optional — it's where the shape comes from.

Starting weight
185–225 lb
This is your anchor lift — log every session. Most people progress to 275+ quickly. If your lower back is doing the work, your feet are too close to your hips — walk them out an inch.
02 Dumbbell RDL 4 × 10· 4-1-X-0· 75s rest· RPE 8
Push hips to the wall behind you — don't pull the weight down
How to do it

Hold a dumbbell in each hand, standing tall, feet hip-width apart. Create a slight knee bend that doesn't change during the movement — this is a hinge, not a squat. Push your hips backward (like trying to close a car door with your butt) while the dumbbells travel down the front of your thighs. Keep your back completely flat and your chest proud. Lower until you feel a deep stretch in your hamstrings — usually mid-shin. Drive your hips forward to return to standing. The 4-second eccentric is where the hamstring sweep gets built.

Starting weight
60–80 lb each hand
Use straps if grip fails before hamstrings — grip is not the target today. Should feel a strong hamstring stretch at the bottom of every rep.
03 Bulgarian Split Squat 15° forward lean 3 × 10 each· 3-1-X-1· 60s rest· RPE 8
Long stride, drive through the front heel — lean forward for glutes not back for quads
How to do it

Stand about 2–3 feet in front of a bench. Place the top of your back foot on the bench, laces down. Hold dumbbells at your sides. Lean your torso forward 15 degrees — this is critical, it's what makes this a glute exercise instead of a quad exercise. Lower until your front thigh is parallel to the floor. Drive through the front heel to stand back up. Your front shin should stay mostly vertical — if your knee shoots way past your toes, step the front foot forward an inch.

Starting weight
30–45 lb DBs each hand
Balance is part of the training stress. If you can't hit 10 strict reps, do bodyweight first until the pattern is grooved. Don't rush the load.
04 Cable Glute Kickback (single-leg) 4 × 12 each· 2-1-1-2· 45s rest· RPE 8
The squeeze and hold is the rep — don't reach for more range with your lower back
How to do it

Attach an ankle strap to a low cable. Face the machine and hold the frame for balance. Strap one ankle. Kick that leg straight back and slightly across your body's center line — the working glute should cross the midline of your standing leg at full extension. Squeeze the glute hard at the top and hold for 2 seconds. Lower under control. The squeeze, not the range, is where the shape comes from. If you feel your lower back arching to get more extension, drop the weight 10 lb.

Starting weight
25–40 lb
Lower than most people start. Strict form first, load second. The 2-second hold is non-negotiable at any weight.
05 Hip Abduction Machine forward lean 4 × 15· 2-0-1-2· 45s rest· RPE 9
Lean 30° forward — that single adjustment changes the target muscle entirely
How to do it

Sit in the abduction machine with knees against the pads. Here's the key: instead of sitting upright (which hits the lower glute), lean your torso forward about 30 degrees. This shifts the target to the gluteus medius — the upper glute that creates the side-shelf look in pants and swimwear. Press your knees apart to full range, brief pause, return slowly. Don't bounce. RPE 9 on the last set means you're 1 rep from failure.

Starting weight
100–140 lb
Machine ranges vary a lot. Go heavy here — this is the upper-glute builder and it needs progressive load to respond. The forward lean alone will make it feel much harder than before.
06 B-Stance Cable RDL novelty 3 × 12 each· 3-1-X-1· 60s rest· RPE 7
80% of your bodyweight on the front leg — back foot is a kickstand, not a co-worker
How to do it

Stand facing a low cable with a rope or D-handle attachment. Put one foot forward, the other 6 inches behind with just the toe touching the floor. Intentionally shift 80% of your bodyweight onto the front leg — the back foot is balance only. Hinge from the hips, lowering the cable while pushing your hips back. Drive through the front heel to return. Keep your back flat throughout. If it feels equal between both legs, you're using the back leg too much — shift forward.

Starting weight
40–60 lb
The single-leg emphasis makes this harder than the load suggests. RPE 7 is intentional — this comes after two heavy hinge movements. Don't go to failure here.
07 isoSingle-Leg Hip Thrust Hold 3 × 25s each· 30s rest
Hips square — the moment one side drops, the rep is over
How to do it

Set up like a standard hip thrust — upper back on the bench, one foot flat on the floor, the other leg extended straight out in front of you. Drive the planted foot down to lift your hips to full extension. Hold the top position for 25 seconds. Critical: keep your hips perfectly square — don't let the unsupported side drop or rotate. That rotation is your glute compensating by cheating. Switch legs. Rest only 30 seconds between sides.

Starting weight
Bodyweight
Add a 25–45 lb plate on your hips when 25 seconds feels easy. Most people don't need added load for the first 4 weeks — just holding the position strictly is the challenge.
05
Finisher
3 min / frog pump burnout — no rest within rounds
→ 2 rounds, no rest within each round

30 frog pumps (1-second top hold every rep) → 15 banded clamshells each side → 30-second plank.

60s rest between rounds.

Frog pumps: Lie on your back, soles of your feet together, knees dropped out to the sides (like a frog). Drive your hips up by squeezing your glutes — hold 1 second at the top. This position engages the glutes at end range of hip external rotation, hitting the lower glute from an angle nothing else in this session does.

Clamshells: Mini-band above the knees. Lie on your side, hips bent 45°, knees bent 90°. Keeping your feet together, raise your top knee as high as you can without rotating your pelvis. 15 reps, switch sides.

Post-Session Conditioning
Immediately after the finisher — don't skip
Z2
Walk

12 minutes · 12% incline · 3.0 mph · treadmill.

Do this while your legs are already fatigued from the session — it costs almost nothing in recovery terms but burns meaningful calories and keeps the glute pump active for another 12 minutes. Don't run. Don't do flat walking. The 12% incline is what keeps the glutes engaged. This is the Tuesday conditioning slot — one of two weekly cardio exposures in the plan.

Skip only if genuine injury — otherwise this is mandatory on Lower A days.
