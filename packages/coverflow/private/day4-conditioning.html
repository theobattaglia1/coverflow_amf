01
Why This Day Exists
Between Pull A and Push B — 48h gap on each side
→ The reasoning

Thursday is sandwiched with 48 hours before Push B and after Pull A. Sled and bike provide a caloric burn and glute activation without touching the pushing or pulling muscles you just trained and are about to train again. It's a conditioning tax with no recovery cost to the vanity muscles.

What you're NOT doing today: HIIT classes, running, spin, anything with random programming or quad-heavy loading. Those burn the wrong currency for this physique and interfere with Friday's delt work.

What you ARE doing: Sled push and pull (pure posterior chain, zero eccentric damage), then Tabata assault bike (anaerobic spike, short duration), then steam. Forty-five minutes total.

04
Session Protocol
Two blocks — sled then bike — then steam
Block A — Sled / 20–25 min
5×
Sled Push + Pull
60 yd push → 60 yd pull → 90s rest

Push (60 yd): Load the sled with a challenging but moveable weight. Lean forward at roughly 45°, short quick steps, driving through the balls of your feet. Arms straight pushing the vertical handles. Don't let your hips rise above your shoulders — power position the whole way.

Pull (60 yd, backward): Grab the rope or straps, lean back slightly, and drive through your heels backward. The backward lean + heel drive forces the glutes to do the work. This is the posterior chain hit — the reason Thursday exists. Walk back to start, rest 90 seconds.

Sled load: challenging but allows you to finish all 5 rounds with good form. If you're grinding to a stop mid-push by round 3, drop 10 lb.

Block B — Assault Bike / 5 min
8×
Tabata Intervals
20s all-out · 10s rest · 8 rounds = 4 min + 1 min cool-down

20 seconds: Full output. Arms and legs driving simultaneously. The assault bike's air resistance means the harder you push, the harder it gets — there's no cheating the effort. Rate of perceived exertion should be 9–10.

10 seconds: Pedal slowly. Don't stop completely — keep blood moving.

Eight rounds of 20/10 = 4 minutes. Spend 1 minute pedaling easy as cool-down. Total: 5 minutes. This is an anaerobic spike that keeps the metabolic rate elevated for hours post-session. Short enough that it doesn't eat into recovery for Push B tomorrow.

If assault bike unavailable: rowing machine Tabata (same protocol) or 5 × 200m sprint with 60s rest.

Block C — Steam Room / 15–20 min
15'
Steam + Cold Contrast
16 oz electrolytes before · 15 min steam · 60–90s cold shower

Before: 16 oz electrolyte water or coconut water. Don't skip this — dehydration blunts the physiological benefit of the steam.

Steam (15 min): Sit or lie in a comfortable position. Breathe through your nose. The heat increases blood flow to the muscles you just worked, helps clear lactic acid, and reduces next-day soreness. You've done sled + bike — the steam earns its place today.

Cold shower (60–90s): As cold as the gym shower goes. The temperature contrast — hot steam to cold water — creates a vascular pumping effect that accelerates recovery more than either alone. You don't have to enjoy it. 60 seconds is enough.
