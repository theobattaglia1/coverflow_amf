02
CORE IDENTITY

THE GOLDEN CHILD WITH GHOSTS

Hudson is the varsity-jacket dream who absorbed every family fracture in silence. Now he alchemizes that backlog into widescreen, heart-punch pop. He still looks like the poster in your locker; he just sings the stuff you never dared to write there.

BRAND PILLARS

HOW WE KNOW WHAT BELONGS

To be the 2020s male equivalent of Taylor Swift meets Springsteen: critically worshipped, culturally dominant, and emotionally indispensable.

Think: If Harry Styles had Taylor Swift's emotional specificity, or if John Mayer had the cultural fluency and visual world-building of Troye Sivan. But less "pretty boy rockstar," more "suburban poet prince."

THEMATIC DNA

Silence as trauma · Masculinity as costume · Suburban safety as illusion · Children translating divorce debris · Grief for a parent still living · Romance as survival mechanism.

Weapon: ruthless specificity without melodrama. "You left a Post-it that said 'don't wait up' / Mom cried into the milk."

03
FAN-DRIVEN RENAISSANCE

WHY HUDSON? — THE OPEN LANE

No male act currently owns the same cultural intersection early-era Taylor Swift did.

No male artist today delivers the exact combination that made early-era Taylor Swift unstoppable: razor-sharp diary lyrics, stadium-scale accessibility, and a universally "safe-to-stan" image. Hudson is engineered to occupy that vacant intersection—confessional enough to tattoo on your heart, broad enough to own pop radio, and polished enough for brands, parents, and critics to embrace.

TAKE-AWAY:

Harry delivers ubiquity + charm but not confessional punch.
Matty delivers confession + artistry but polarizes the mainstream.
No one combines all three.

Hudson's thesis is to close that triangle:
Poster-boy approachability + diaristic heartbreak + arena-ready hooks—without losing critical credibility.

06
SOUND & VOICE

ANTHEMIC. INTIMATE. UNMISTAKABLY HIM.

Indie-adjacent pop with Americana bloodlines. The production touchstones are Bleachers, The 1975, Jon Brion, Aaron Dessner, and Ruston Kelly, with the structural ambition of *Channel Orange*. The vocal is natural and unpretentious—low cracks invite you in, belts feel earned.

LYRICAL POV

Hyper-emotional, self-effacing, intimate without being melodramatic. Writes like he's texting an ex at 2am but crafts it like poetry. Topics: nostalgia, masculinity, being misunderstood, unreturned texts, hometown guilt, getting older at 23.

SONIC PALETTE
  • The 1975 – anthemic bite
  • Folklore-era Swift – diaristic detail
  • Sam Fender – heartland grit
  • Bruce Springsteen – timeless storytelling
  • John Mayer – melodic craftsmanship

COVER ART SYSTEM

ONE UNIFIED SYSTEM
ACROSS THE TRILOGY

Every Act gets a single "totem" object that sums up that chapter of Hudson's journey.

SINGLES

Wide, cinematic environmental shots: the object dominates the frame; Hudson is distant or off-center.

EP ARTWORK

Flips the perspective: a tight portrait of Hudson alongside / around / in front of a miniature replica of that same scene. (Working concept).

OBJECT FUNCTION

Objects double as merch icons, stage props, and lore breadcrumbs, tying the trilogy together visually and narratively.

RESULT:

Each rollout feels cohesive (fans spot the object instantly) yet delivers a visual "pay-off" when the EP drops.

VIDEO STRATEGY

"PHYSICALISE THE FEELING,
DON'T ILLUSTRATE THE PLOT"

Hudson videos should hit you in the chest within ten seconds, using practical effects, single-take tricks, miniatures, forced perspective, and lo-fi shooting formats (Super 8, DV, steady handheld). They must be easy to describe, easy to GIF, and impossible to forget.

FEELING > PLOT

We are not trying to jam in an over-ambitious narrative - we just want to capture and convey a feeling.

ONE-LINE HOOK

Simple enough conceptually that you could describe it in one sentence.

LO-FI PROD / HIGH-CONCEPT IDEAS

Shoot scrappy but think creatively - turn the challenge of keeping budget tight into an opportunity to elevate things conceptually. Practical effects, real props, hidden cuts, projector tricks, miniature sets, colour-gel headlights—ideas that look expensive because they're clever.

REPEATABLE RITUAL

Fans should recognise a Hudson video in two frames: blue-leaning grade, tactile prop, single motif that ties back to the EP object (car, moving box, porch light).

08
WORLD-BUILDING

LORE IS THE
LOYALTY ENGINE

The music hits. The world lingers. That's the strategy.

THE PHILOSOPHY

In a landscape flooded with content, context is the differentiator. Hudson doesn't just release music — he constructs a narrative ecosystem fans can live inside.

THE SYSTEM

Every non-musical touchpoint plays one role: To deepen the mystery, not solve it:

  • The Feeds: Cinematic lyric stills, voicemail audio drops, driving POV clips
  • Live Shows: Mystery voicemail intros, symbolic objects, mic-less intimate songs
  • Physical Objects: Limited merch drops that feel thrifted, not branded
