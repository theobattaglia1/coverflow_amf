Hudson Ingram

Artist • Interscope/Dessner JV

Hudson Ingram, an 18-year-old Nashville-based artist from Texas, makes music that feels as big as commercial pop but is built on a foundation of sharp songwriting and real musicianship. The son of country artist Jack Ingram, he grew up immersed in storytelling, developing a style that merges intimate, deeply felt lyrics with hooks that hit on first listen. Obsessed with the craft, self-taught in production, and relentless in his pursuit of mastery, Hudson is carving his own lane—one that feels classic, yet completely his own.

Jack Schrepferman

Producer • Kobalt

Jack Schrepferman is a Nashville-based multi genre songwriter, producer, and multi-instrumentalist known for crafting song-centered music that seamlessly blends pop sensibility with bold, innovative production. At just 21, his catalogue has amassed over 70 million streams across all genres, with recent singles cut by Dasha (Warner), Dhruv (RCA), Steinza (Santa Ana), Midwxst (Geffen), and Astrid S (Island). Jack spent his formative years working closely with Grammy-nominated, multi-platinum producer JT Daly, contributing to projects for artists such as Benson Boone, Tai Verdes, Bully, and PVRIS at MMK Studios. This experience laid the foundation for Jack's adaptable and artist-first approach, regardless of musical style. Jack's work has also included placements for brands like Michael Kors and Amazon. He is actively collaborating with both emerging and established artists / writers, including Jensen McRae, Johnny Orlando, M-Phazes, Geoff Warburton alongside developing sounds with rising independent talents such as Hudson Ingram, Ryman, Conall Cafferty, Kenzie Cait, and Casper Sage.

Ruby Plume

Artist/Songwriter

Ruby Plume is an alt-folk-pop artist from Boston whose intricate storytelling and lush guitar melodies form a liminal, daydreamy musical canvas. Her vocals carry a quiet intensity, layering youthful clarity over emotional depth reminiscent of artists who defined alt-pop for a new generation. Ruby's songs are subtly prismatic—shifting effortlessly between vivid emotion and elusive mystery, warmth and introspection. Beyond her standout singer-songwriter talent, Ruby is a poly-artistic force, incorporating guitar finesse, imaginative illustration, and mixed-media art into her creative arsenal. These visual explorations deepen the vivid daydream that is her developing world—rounding out a creative universe that feels both tangible and dreamlike.

Conall Cafferty

Artist/Songwriter

Conall Cafferty—a 20-year-old singer-songwriter from Montclair, NJ now rooted in Nashville—pairs a self-taught, blues-bred guitar style and distinctive vocal tone with the sharp lyricism he honed during a stint at Belmont's songwriting program and a brief turn on American Idol. His songs fuse porch-born intimacy with the kind of hooks that can fill a room, reflecting influences that range from B.B. King to Damien Rice while never losing their raw, first-take authenticity. Recent co-writes with Jonas Connor, Jack Schrepferman, Jake Finch, and Collin Pastore show Conall carving a lane where indie-twinged Americana feels both deeply personal and ready for the biggest stages.

Kate Stephenson

Artist/Creator

Kate Stephenson is a Nashville-based folk-pop experimentalist from the Illinois suburbs. Raised backstage while her orchestral-musician parents rehearsed and classically trained from childhood, she carries that precision in her bones—then deliberately bends it into spellbinding shapes, using detuned guitars, off-kilter drum loops, and cinematic lyric lines to conjure something unmistakably her own. Her work lives where confessional storytelling meets sonic risk: finger-picked melodies collide with glitchy samples, strings swell into sudden dissonance, and off-the-cuff humor disarms before the next emotional gut punch lands. Recording, mixing, and mutating most tracks herself, Stephenson prizes autonomy over polish, chasing the imperfect take that feels undeniable. The result is a daring, true-artist catalogue that stretches folk honesty across pop's elastic edges while always keeping the listener within whispered reach.

Leon Sharplin

Producer/Artist

Leon Sharplin is a Los Angeles–based producer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist from New Zealand whose Berklee-honed chops let him weave finger-picked guitars, chamber-ready strings, and surgically sculpted synths into one seamless folk-pop-R&B mosaic. His "special sauce" is intentional contrast: live instruments tracked with audiophile care, then bent through sleek electronic sound design until the familiar feels future-bound yet instantly cohesive. In the studio, the 22-year-old leaves ego at the door and zeroes in on amplifying an artist's own voice—whether that means ghosting behind the console, layering harmony on a vintage bass, or deconstructing a beat until it breathes. A former metal guitarist who detoured through jazz and hip-hop, Sharplin now carries a genre-fluid toolkit and an obsessive attention to detail that turns half-formed sketches into fully realized worlds. The through-line is always the same: serve the song, push the edges, and make the atypical sound inevitable.
