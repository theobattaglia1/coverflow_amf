CONALL
CAFFERTY

Artist/Songwriter

Conall Cafferty—a 20-year-old singer-songwriter from Montclair, NJ now rooted in Nashville—pairs a self-taught, blues-bred guitar style and distinctive vocal tone with the sharp lyricism he honed during a stint at Belmont's songwriting program and a brief turn on American Idol. His songs fuse porch-born intimacy with the kind of hooks that can fill a room, reflecting influences that range from B.B. King to Damien Rice while never losing their raw, first-take authenticity. Recent co-writes with Jonas Connor, Jack Schrepferman, Jake Finch, and Collin Pastore show Conall carving a lane where indie-twinged Americana feels both deeply personal and ready for the biggest stages.

KATE
STEPHENSON

Artist/Creator

Kate Stephenson is a Nashville-based folk-pop experimentalist from the Illinois suburbs. Raised backstage while her orchestral-musician parents rehearsed and classically trained from childhood, she carries that precision in her bones—then deliberately bends it into spellbinding shapes, using detuned guitars, off-kilter drum loops, and cinematic lyric lines to conjure something unmistakably her own.

Her work lives where confessional storytelling meets sonic risk: finger-picked melodies collide with glitchy samples, strings swell into sudden dissonance, and off-the-cuff humor disarms before the next emotional gut punch lands. Recording, mixing, and mutating most tracks herself, Stephenson prizes autonomy over polish, chasing the imperfect take that feels undeniable.

TOM
SILETTO

Artist

Tom Siletto is an emerging artist in the indie folk/pop scene whose introspective, candid songwriting draws listeners in as if in conversation with a close friend. He seamlessly blends strong lyricism and haunting instrumentation, evoking the gloomy cliffs of Northern California where he grew up.

Siletto is also part of the duo Darling, which has amassed devoted listeners after just two released songs. Their first single, 'couldn't i,' garnered about 200k streams on Spotify alone in the first month, without help from editorial playlists.

LEON
SHARPLIN

Songwriter/Producer

Leon Sharplin is a Los Angeles–based producer, songwriter, and multi-instrumentalist from New Zealand whose Berklee-honed chops let him weave finger-picked guitars, chamber-ready strings, and surgically sculpted synths into one seamless folk-pop-R&B mosaic. His "special sauce" is intentional contrast: live instruments tracked with audiophile care, then bent through sleek electronic sound design until the familiar feels future-bound yet instantly cohesive.

In the studio, the 22-year-old leaves ego at the door and zeroes in on amplifying an artist's own voice—whether that means ghosting behind the console, layering harmony on a vintage bass, or deconstructing a beat until it breathes. A former metal guitarist who detoured through jazz and hip-hop, Sharplin now carries a genre-fluid toolkit and an obsessive attention to detail that turns half-formed sketches into fully realized worlds.
